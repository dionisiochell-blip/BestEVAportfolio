About

I've sat inside the chaos I now automate.

For the last few years I've been the operations layer behind founders and executives — filtering inboxes, running calendars, chasing follow-ups, and keeping CRMs honest. Selected builds

Four systems, four different bottlenecks.

Zapier + MakeAI ContentMulti-platform

One recording becomes a week of content

Drive upload → transcript → blog + social → published ×3

A Drive trigger catches new .mp4 files. AI transcribes and drafts blog posts, then branches into platform paths — AI-generated images per post, with Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn publishing once content clears review. Built twice: once in Zapier, once in Make.

One upload now produces a transcript, blog posts, social images, and published posts across three platforms — zero copy-pasting.
MakeAI AgentFacebook Pages

Messages get answered before the team even sees them

Webhook → Gemini agent + knowledge base → reply

A webhook catches incoming Page messages and hands them to an AI agent trained on the company's own knowledge base, with memory to keep conversations coherent, then sends the reply back automatically.

Common questions get an instant, on-brand answer around the clock — the team only steps in for what needs a person.
ZapierAsanaCRM

Every lead stage has a job to do, automatically

Stage change → its own follow-up path ×5

Updating a lead's status now triggers its own path: a lead folder and content task on kickoff, a two-step nurture sequence on no response, quote follow-ups, a welcome email and PDF on approval, and a referral ask once paid and closed.

Every stage follows up on itself — nothing waits on someone's memory.
ZapierApolloLead Routing

Leads arrive already qualified

New lead → Apollo enrich → priority split → Slack + draft

A webhook catches the new lead, pulls the company URL, enriches it through Apollo, then splits by priority — high-priority leads get saved, Slacked to sales, and an AI-drafted outreach email; lower-priority leads get a quieter notification.

Sales opens a Slack message already knowing who the lead is — with an email already drafted.
